Step 1 - You can do it

First things first, you need to acknowledge that you can fold your phone. Secondly, don't give up or compromise the experience by doing the steps halfway. Go all the way if you're committed and stick through it for at least 1 month. If by the end you aren't happy with folding your phone download all your apps again.


Take a deep breath. you can do it.

Step 2 - Increase friction to open your phone

I know that Apple, Samsung, and Google spent millions of dollars on fingerprint technology, but I want you to remove all instances of your finger print. Go back to 2012 when we had to PIN or swipe in order to get into our phones.

Step 3 - Goodbye Infinite content


Get rid of all of the apps that have this kind of interaction. With this type of mechanism, our nature is to constantly check our phone and open 10 apps with every phone pickup hoping that we get something new each time.

Let me put it in an analogy: Is it better to eat a bowl of rice one grain at a time every second, or eat however you want?


Example Apps to delete: Gmail, Email, Facebook, snapchat, Instagram, Twitter, Tumblr, Pinterest, etc.

Step 4 - Goodbye Infinite content remedy

Deleting those apps isn't the only step involved - there's one more. You have to disable safari and delete all other web browsers. That way, you'll increase friction and the effort required to type in "instagram.com" in your mobile browser

Disabling Safari how to: Go to Settings -> Go to Restrictions -> Turn on Restrictions -> Turn off Safari.

Step 5 - You've folded your phone

You did it. You folded your phone. Much like folding a piece of paper, your phone is still there and it still serves your purposes. But the only difference is that you are in control. I encourage you to fold your phone for at least 1 month before installing everything again.
